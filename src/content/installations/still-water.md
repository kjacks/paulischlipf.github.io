# Still Water

Two hundred circular mirrors hang at varying heights from the ceiling of a subterranean carpark. A single projector casts a time-lapse sky across them — clouds drifting, light changing from dawn to dusk in forty minutes.

Drivers and pedestrians encounter the sky underground. The familiar is made strange; orientation is gently confused. The work is most effective when discovered accidentally.
